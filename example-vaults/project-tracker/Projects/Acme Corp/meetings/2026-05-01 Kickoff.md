## Current state (from Sam)

The application is a 10-year-old Rails monolith running on EC2 instances behind an ALB. No containers anywhere. They deploy via Capistrano. Biggest pain points:

- Deploys take 45 minutes and are nerve-wracking
- Can't scale individual services independently
- Dev/prod parity is poor — lots of "works on my machine" bugs

Database is PostgreSQL on RDS — that part is actually fine. Main migration concern is the application tier.

## Scope confirmed

12-week engagement. Our deliverables:

1. Architecture document (week 2)
2. Migration runbook (week 4)
3. First service migrated and running in containers (week 8)
4. Handoff documentation + knowledge transfer sessions (weeks 10–12)

## Key decisions

**Container orchestration: ECS Fargate** — Jordan was open to either ECS or K8s. We recommended Fargate: the team has no K8s experience and adding the operational burden of a cluster wasn't justified for their scale. Jordan agreed.

**First migration candidate: reporting service** — lower traffic (~500 req/day), fewer dependencies than the main application, good test case. Sam identified this. Taylor flagged that the finance team relies on it for month-end reports — confirm migration isn't scheduled near reporting cycles.
